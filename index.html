Guide to painting your house

Painting your house is a straightforward DIY project that starts with thorough preparation, quality materials, and proper technique to achieve a professional finish that lasts for years. Whether you're tackling an interior paint colors refresh, working with best exterior paint brands, or planning a larger project, this guide walks you through the essentials step by step.

Step-by-Step Painting Guide

1. Choose paint type and finish

paint finishes guide

Selecting the right sheen is just as important as the color itself. We recommend a durable semi-gloss for high-traffic areas like kitchens, while a flat or eggshell finish provides a sophisticated look for bedrooms and living areas.

2. Inspect walls for damage

wall damage repair

Before opening a single can of paint, walk your perimeter to look for water stains, peeling, or deep dents. Identifying these issues early ensures your foundation is sound and prevents moisture from ruining your new coat later.

3. Clean surfaces thoroughly

wall cleaning tips

Dust and oils are the enemies of paint adhesion. A quick wipe-down with a damp cloth or a mild TSP solution removes hidden grime, ensuring your primer bonds perfectly to the surface for a flake-free finish.

4. Patch holes and cracks

spackle technique

Use a high-quality spackle to fill in nail holes and hairline fractures, then sand them flush once dry. This extra bit of effort eliminates "shadows" on the wall, resulting in a perfectly smooth, gallery-like appearance.

5. Apply painter's tape

painter's tape tips

Achieving those razor-sharp lines requires a steady hand and premium painter's tape. Apply the tape in short sections to prevent stretching, and use a putty knife to press the edges down firmly to stop any paint bleed.

6. Prime surfaces

priming techniques

Primer acts as the "glue" between your wall and your paint while also blocking old colors from bleeding through. It’s a non-negotiable step if you’re painting over a dark color or working with fresh drywall.

7. Paint first coat

painting techniques

Start by "cutting in" around the edges with a brush before using a roller for the large sections. Focus on maintaining a "wet edge" to avoid lap marks, and don't worry if the first coat looks a little thin—the magic happens in the next step.

8. Apply second coat & finish

painting techniques

The second coat provides the true depth of color and the final layer of protection for your walls. Once dry, carefully remove your painter's tape at a 45-degree angle to reveal those satisfying, professional lines.
